Compound Interest Questions

1. A bank offers 5per compound interest calculated on half-yearly basis. A customer deposits

Rs. 1600 each on 1st January and 1st July of a year. At the end of the year, the amount he

would have gained by way of interest is:

A.Rs. 120

B.Rs. 121

C.Rs. 122

D.Rs. 123

Answer: Option B



2. The difference between simple and compound interests compounded annually on a certain

sum of money for 2 years at 4per per annum is Re. 1. The sum (in Rs.) is:

A.625

B.630

C.640

D.650

Answer: Option A



3. There is 60per increase in an amount in 6 years at simple interest. What will be the

compound interest of Rs. 12,000 after 3 years at the same rate?

A.Rs. 2160

B.Rs. 3120

C.Rs. 3972

D.Rs. 6240

E. None of these

Answer: Option C



4. What is the difference between the compound interests on Rs. 5000 for 1 years at 4per

per annum compounded yearly and half-yearly?

A.Rs. 2.04

B.Rs. 3.06

C.Rs. 4.80

D.Rs. 8.30

Answer: Option A



5. The compound interest on Rs. 30,000 at 7per per annum is Rs. 4347. The period (in years) is:

A.2

B.212

C.3

D.4

Answer: Option A
