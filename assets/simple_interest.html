Simple Interest

1. Principal : The money borrowed or lent out for a certain period is called the principal or the sum.

2. Interest : E*tra money paid for using other's money is called interest.

3. Simple Interest (S.I.) : If the interest on a sum borrowed for certain period is reckoned uniformly, then it is called simple interest.

   Let Principal = P, Rate = Rper per annum (p.a.) and Time = T years.

   Then

(i) Simple Intereest = (P * R * T)/100.

(ii) P = ((100 * S.I)/(R * T)), R = ((100 * S.I.)/(P * T)) and T = ((100 * S.I.)/(P * R))s .
