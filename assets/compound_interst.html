Compound Interest formula

1. Let Principal = P, Rate = Rper per annum, Time = n years.

2. When interest is compound Annually:

            Amount = P(1+(R/100))n

3. When interest is compounded Half-yearly:

            Amount = P(1+((R/2)/100))2n

4. When interest is compounded Quarterly:

            Amount = P(1+((R/4)/100))4n

5. When interest is compounded Annually

but time is in fraction, say 3.5 years.

            Amount =P(1+(R/100))3*(1+(((2/5)R)/100))

6. When Rates are different for different years,

say R1per, R2per, R3per for 1st, 2nd and 3rd year respectively.

            Then, Amount = P(1+(R1/100))(1+(R2/100))(1+(R3/100))

7. Present worth of Rs. x due n years hence is given by:

            Present Worth =(x/(1+(R/100)))
