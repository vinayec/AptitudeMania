Percentage Formula

1. Concept of Percentage:

By a certain percent, we mean that many hundredths.

Thus, x percent means x hundredths, written as xper.

To express xper as a fraction: We have, xper = (x/100).

Thus, 20per =(20/100)=1/5. To express (a/b) as a percent:

We have, (a/b)=((a/b)*100)per

Thus,(1/4)=((1/4)*100)per=25per

2. Percentage Increase/Decrease:

If the price of a commodity increases by Rper,

then the reduction in consumption so as not to increase the expenditure is:

            ((R/(100+R))*100)per

If the price of a commodity decreases by Rper,

then the increase in consumption so

as not to decrease the expenditure is:

            ((R/(100-R))*100)per

3. Results on Population:

Let the population of a town be P now and

suppose it increases at the rate of Rper per annum, then:

                        1. Population after n years = P(1+(R/100))n

                        2. Population n years ago =(P/(1+(R/100)))n

4. Results on Depreciation:

Let the present value of a machine be P.

Suppose it depreciates at the rate of Rper per annum. Then:

                        1. Value of the machine after n years = P((1-(R/100)))n

                        2. Value of the machine n years ago = (P/(1-(R/100)))n

                        3. If A is Rper more than B, then B is less than A by ((R/(100+R))*100)per

                        4. If A is Rper less than B, then B is more than A by ((R/(100-R))*100)per
